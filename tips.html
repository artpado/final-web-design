Essential Tips

Get your pan very hot

Most stir-fry dishes need high heat. A lukewarm pan steams instead of frying. Heat the pan first, then add oil — you should see light smoke before adding any ingredient.

Balance your flavours

Asian cooking relies on salty (soy sauce, fish sauce), sweet (sugar, mirin), sour (lime, rice vinegar) and umami (miso, oyster sauce). Taste as you go and adjust — there is no single correct ratio.

Rinse your rice

Rinse white rice 2–3 times in cold water before cooking. It removes excess starch and gives you separate, fluffy grains instead of a sticky clump. Let it rest covered for 10 minutes after cooking.

Add fresh herbs at the end

Coriander, basil, mint and spring onion lose their fragrance when cooked. Add them just before serving so their flavour stays bright.
