Shoyu Ramen

Prep: 10 min  |  Cook: 40 min  |  Serves: 2

Ingredients
  • 2 portions fresh or dried ramen noodles
  • 900 ml chicken stock
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 slices nori (dried seaweed)
  • 60 g canned bamboo shoots, drained
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
Steps
  1. Soft-boil the eggs for exactly 7 minutes. Transfer to cold water, peel and set aside.
  2. Bring stock to a gentle simmer. Add soy sauce, mirin and sesame oil. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  3. Cook noodles separately according to packet instructions (usually 3–4 min). Drain and divide into two bowls.
  4. Pour the hot broth over the noodles. Halve the eggs and place them on top.
  5. Add bamboo shoots, a sheet of nori and sliced spring onion. Serve immediately.
Pad Thai

Prep: 15 min  |  Cook: 15 min  |  Serves: 2

Ingredients
  • 180 g flat rice noodles (5 mm wide)
  • 150 g raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 80 g firm tofu, cubed
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 50 g bean sprouts
  • 30 g roasted peanuts, roughly crushed
  • 1 lime, cut in wedges
Steps
  1. Soak noodles in warm water for 20 minutes until pliable. Drain well.
  2. Mix tamarind paste, fish sauce and sugar in a small bowl. Set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Fry garlic for 30 seconds, then add shrimp and tofu. Cook 2–3 min until shrimp are pink.
  4. Push everything to the side. Crack eggs into the wok and scramble until just set, then mix with the rest.
  5. Add drained noodles and pour the sauce over. Toss everything together for 2 minutes.
  6. Serve with bean sprouts, crushed peanuts and lime wedges on the side.
Pan-Fried Dumplings

Prep: 25 min  |  Cook: 12 min  |  Serves: 2 (16 dumplings)

Ingredients
  • 16 round dumpling wrappers (store-bought)
  • 200 g ground pork
  • 2 tsp fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 2 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil for frying
  • 80 ml water
Steps
  1. Mix pork, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, vinegar and spring onions until well combined.
  2. Place 1 heaped teaspoon of filling in the centre of each wrapper. Wet the edges with water, fold and press firmly to seal.
  3. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Place dumplings flat-side down. Cook 3 minutes until the bottoms are golden.
  4. Pour in the water and immediately cover with a lid. Steam for 6–7 minutes until the water has evaporated.
  5. Remove lid and cook 1 more minute to re-crisp the bottoms. Serve with soy sauce and chili oil.
Miso Soup

Prep: 5 min  |  Cook: 8 min  |  Serves: 2

Ingredients
  • 600 ml water
  • 1 tsp dashi powder (or 1 dashi sachet)
  • 2 tbsp white miso paste (shiro miso)
  • 150 g silken tofu, cut into 1 cm cubes
  • 1 tbsp dried wakame seaweed
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
Steps
  1. Rehydrate the wakame in cold water for 5 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Bring water to a simmer and dissolve the dashi powder. Add tofu and wakame. Heat for 2 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat. Scoop out a small amount of hot broth, dissolve the miso paste into it, then stir it back into the pot. Do not boil after adding miso — it loses its flavour.
  4. Ladle into bowls and top with spring onion. Serve straight away.
Korean Bulgogi

Prep: 10 min + 30 min marinating  |  Cook: 8 min  |  Serves: 2

Ingredients
  • 300 g beef sirloin or ribeye, sliced as thin as possible (freeze for 30 min first to make slicing easier)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp Asian pear or apple, grated (tenderises the meat)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 spring onions, sliced, to serve
Steps
  1. Mix soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, grated pear, garlic and ginger in a bowl.
  2. Add the beef slices and mix well. Cover and marinate for at least 30 minutes in the fridge (overnight is better).
  3. Heat a pan or griddle over very high heat. Cook the beef in a single layer for 2–3 minutes each side. Do not overcrowd the pan — cook in batches if needed.
  4. Serve over steamed rice, topped with spring onion and sesame seeds.
Beef Phở

Prep: 10 min  |  Cook: 35 min  |  Serves: 2

Ingredients
  • 180 g flat rice noodles (banh pho)
  • 150 g beef sirloin, sliced paper-thin
  • 900 ml good beef stock
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cloves
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Fresh bean sprouts, Thai basil, mint, lime and sliced chili to serve
Steps
  1. Toast the star anise, cinnamon and cloves in a dry pan for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Add beef stock, fish sauce and sugar. Simmer for 25 minutes. Strain the broth and discard the spices.
  3. Soak noodles in hot water for 15 minutes, or cook according to packet. Drain and divide into bowls.
  4. Arrange raw beef slices over the noodles. Pour the boiling hot broth over — the heat will cook the beef in about 1 minute.
  5. Serve immediately with bean sprouts, herbs, lime and chili on the side.
